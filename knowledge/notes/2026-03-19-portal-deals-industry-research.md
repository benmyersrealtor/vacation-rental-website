# Industry Research: Zillow/Keller Williams, eXp/Homes.com, Compass/Redfin

_Date saved: 2026-03-19_

## Summary

In March 2026, residential real estate portals and major brokerages accelerated a shift toward **premarket / coming-soon listing distribution**.

Three related developments matter most:

1. **Zillow + Keller Williams (plus other launch partners)**
   - Zillow launched **Zillow Preview**, allowing participating brokerages' coming-soon listings to appear on Zillow and Trulia before hitting the MLS active market.
   - Launch partners reported in coverage included **Keller Williams, REMAX, HomeServices of America, Side, and United Real Estate**.
   - Strategic significance: gives brokerages a listing-presentation and recruiting advantage while helping Zillow retain control of consumer discovery.

2. **eXp + Homes.com / Realtor.com / ComeHome**
   - eXp announced a competing premarket syndication arrangement with **Homes.com, Realtor.com, and ComeHome.com**.
   - eXp framed the move as **non-exclusive** and positioned it as broad-distribution, seller-visibility, and MLS-compliant.
   - Strategic significance: eXp is avoiding portal dependence on one platform while ensuring it is not left out of the premarket inventory race.

3. **Compass + Redfin**
   - Compass had previously partnered with **Redfin** to distribute Compass coming-soon inventory.
   - That move appears to have accelerated Zillow’s response.
   - Compass later dropped its lawsuit against Zillow after Zillow revised its position and no longer banned listings marketed on public-facing portals like Redfin.
   - Strategic significance: Compass helped trigger the market shift, but competitors quickly copied and broadened the model.

## Analysis

### Core takeaway
The competitive fight is shifting from **who has listings** to **who gets listings first**.

### Why Zillow/Keller Williams matters most
- Zillow still has the strongest consumer traffic and brand reach.
- If KW agents can promise premarket exposure on Zillow/Trulia, that becomes a meaningful recruiting and seller-pitch advantage.
- Zillow gets to defend a pro-transparency narrative while still adapting to off-MLS behavior.

### Why eXp’s move matters
- eXp’s portal strategy appears broader and less exclusive.
- It positions eXp as more open and less portal-captive than a single-platform deal.
- It may be strategically weaker than Zillow’s audience advantage, but stronger as a hedge.

### Why Compass/Redfin mattered first
- Compass made premarket listing distribution a major strategic issue.
- Redfin gave Compass a public-facing portal outlet for that inventory.
- Zillow and eXp then responded with their own frameworks.

## Implications for brokerages

### 1. Premarket inventory is now a battleground
Coming-soon inventory is no longer a niche tactic. It is becoming part of brokerage strategy, recruiting, listing presentations, and portal competition.

### 2. Portals are competing for early inventory access
The key question is no longer only who displays listings, but who displays them **before the MLS active date**.

### 3. MLSs may be pressured to adapt
As brokerages and portals create alternate premarket channels, MLSs risk becoming the later-stage distribution layer unless they respond with clearer strategy or product evolution.

### 4. Recruiting implications are real
Brokerages can now pitch agents with early-exposure inventory advantages on major portals. That matters in recruiting and retention.
